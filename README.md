Group Topic Theme: **Historical computing** 

Throughout history, people have looked for ways to secure sensitive information.  Early methods of encryption began with classical ciphers such as the Caesar cipher. However, as communication became more important, encryption methods also became more advanced. The codebreaking methods used before became time-consuming and inefficient as these systems became harder and more complicated to decipher. This led mathematicians and engineers to develop automated and mechanical solutions for encryption and codebreaking. In the early twentieth century, the development of polyalphabetic ciphers eventually contributed to the creation of electromechanical rotor machines. This includes the Hebern Rotor Machine and Arthur Scherbius's Enigma Machine. 

This virtual exhibit project mainly focuses on cryptography and its relationship with the development of computing technologies. The increasing complexity of cryptographic systems before and during World War II made encrypted communications more difficult to decipher. This led to the development of specialized machines such as the Bombe and Colossus. By tracing the history of cryptography from classical ciphers to the Digital and Network Age, the project will explore how the growing challenges of encryption and codebreaking led to the development of specialized machines. These innovations eventually evolved into early general-purpose computers like the Harvard Mark I, ENIAC, and UNIVAC, helping shape modern computing. The exhibit will also highlight how these developments laid the foundation for modern encryption systems and internet security technologies that continue to protect information today. 

The proposed topic will include the following interactive elements: 

- **User Simulation** - The users will be able to simulate the operation of a Rotor Encryption module visually demonstrating how mechanical rotors laid the groundwork for early computing. Users interact with a simplified three-rotor model, customizing numeric offsets to observe how minor configuration shifts drastically alter encrypted outputs. Ultimately, this hands-on simulation highlights how the mathematical complexity of early cryptography necessitated the creation of programmable codebreaking machines. 

- **Interactive Historical Timeline** - To guide users through the evolution of secure communications, the project utilizes a vertically scrolling historical timeline. This design choice allows for chronological tracking from c. 50 BCE through to the modern Internet Security Age. The timeline categorizes over 2,000 years of history into four distinct, filterable eras: 

   - Classical: Highlighting algorithmic transformations like the Caesar Cipher. 

   - Mechanical: Covering the shift to polyalphabetic and rotor-based automation. 

   - Wartime Computing: Focusing on how cryptanalysis at Bletchley Park forced the invention of early electronic computers. 

   - Digital & Network Age: Showcasing how cryptography became the foundation for modern web trust and personal encryption. 

- **Codebreaking Challenge** - Users will be presented with encrypted messages and clues, allowing them to experience the challenges faced by wartime cryptanalysts and gain a better understanding of cryptographic principles. Using the knowledge gained, the users must attempt to decipher the messages within a limited number of attempts, gradually increasing in difficulty, introducing different concepts the higher the difficulty.

## **UI & Interactive Guidelines** 

- **User Simulation:** Mechanical keystroke animations with glowing paths tracing the signal through the simplified rotor-based cipher system. 

- **Rotor Settings:** Coded to allow for modifying of rotor positions. 

- **Timeline Layout:** Horizontal path with clickable circular nodes for exploring key events. 

- **Timeline Pop-ups:** Clean modal cards displaying historical images, diagrams, and notable figures. 

- **Codebreaking Challenge:** Clues styled as classified manila dossiers. 

- **Codebreaking Inputs:** Terminal-style text fields for attempting to decipher the messages. 

- **Difficulty Scaling:** Subtle visual static added to the background as cryptographic concepts increase in difficulty.
